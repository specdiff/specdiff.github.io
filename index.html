Overview

What is speculative diffusion decoding?

Speculative decoding is a draft-then-verify framework: a fast drafter proposes a short continuation, and a large verifier checks those tokens in parallel. If the draft is likely under the verifier, you accept a long prefix in one shot.

SpecDiff replaces the autoregressive drafter with a masked discrete diffusion model that drafts an entire window of tokens in parallel via iterative denoising. This removes a major bottleneck: drafting no longer requires token-by-token generation.

What does SpecDiff-2 add?

SpecDiff-2 focuses on a core practical issue: drafter–verifier alignment. Diffusion drafters can be extremely parallel, but if they propose tokens the verifier often rejects, speed-up evaporates.

  • Train-time alignment: streak-distillation to optimize for long accepted prefixes.
  • Test-time alignment: self-selection over multiple parallel drafts to maximize expected throughput.
  • Scaling insight: “acceleration–compute” scaling links faster decoding to better performance under fixed time budgets.

Blog

Two-part walkthrough: first the core SpecDiff idea, then SpecDiff-2’s alignment improvements and the strongest results.

Part I

SpecDiff: diffusion as a speculative drafter

Motivation

Standard decoding is sequential: the verifier produces one token at a time. Speculative decoding reduces verifier work by verifying a block of tokens at once — but many systems still use an autoregressive drafter, which remains sequential and limits parallelism.

Key idea

Use a masked discrete diffusion language model to draft a window of γ tokens in parallel. Drafting cost is driven mostly by the number of denoising steps, not the draft length.

How verification works (high level)

  1. The diffusion drafter proposes γ candidate tokens (all positions at once).
  2. The target LLM computes probabilities for those positions in parallel.
  3. Accept the longest prefix of the draft that passes the acceptance rule; then continue.

Takeaway

SpecDiff shows diffusion drafters can deliver large speed-ups when they remain sufficiently aligned to the verifier — and it highlights alignment as the key lever to push performance further.

Part II

SpecDiff-2: scaling alignment (and scaling wins)

The bottleneck: misalignment

A diffusion drafter can be fast, but if its proposed tokens are frequently rejected by the verifier, the system spends time drafting tokens that never get used. SpecDiff-2 makes alignment a first-class objective.

Train-time alignment: streak-distillation

Rather than optimizing token-wise match, streak-distillation targets contiguous accepted prefixes. Intuitively: maximize the expected length of the accepted “streak” so each verifier call yields more committed tokens.

Test-time alignment: self-selection acceptance

SpecDiff-2 leverages diffusion’s ability to sample multiple drafts cheaply. From parallel candidates, it selects the one with the highest expected throughput under the verifier, then verifies only that choice.

Why the strongest results matter

Beyond raw speed-ups, SpecDiff-2 emphasizes that faster inference can translate into higher task accuracy under fixed time budgets (e.g., spending saved time on longer reasoning chains).

Results snapshot (as reported)

  • Average speed-up: 4.22× without quality loss; up to 5.5×.
  • Throughput vs prior baselines: +55%.
  • Math-500: 4.71× average speed-up (vs EAGLE-2’s 3.43×).
  • GPQA: 3.24× speed-up (vs EAGLE-2’s 2.80×).
  • Late-position acceptance: +3.2× at later draft positions (from streak-distillation analysis).
  • Multi-draft scaling: up to +20% throughput with 8 parallel drafts (temperature-tuned).
